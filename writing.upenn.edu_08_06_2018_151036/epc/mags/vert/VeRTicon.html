sub·vert (sb-vûrt)


      v. tr. sub·vert·ed, sub·vert·ing,
      sub·verts.


      
1.To destroy completely;
      ruin: "schemes to subvert the liberties of a great community"

      (Alexander Hamilton). 

      2.To undermine the character, morals, or allegiance of; corrupt.


      3.To overthrow completely: "Economic assistance . . . must
      subvert the existing . . . feudal or tribal order" (Henry
      A. Kissinger). See Synonyms at overthrow.


      
[Middle English subverten,
      from Old French subvertir, from Latin subvertere: sub-, sub-
      + vertere, to turn; see wer-2 in Indo-European Roots.]
