NOTES AND ACKNOWLEDGMENTS
 
        In March 1996, shortly after Larry Eigner's death, the
        spontaneous appearance of a number of tributes to Larry's work —
        ported now at the Electronic Poetry Center — led us to set aside a
        future issue of Passages for further responses and memorials.  We
        circulated invitations on the Internet and posted an announcement to
        Poetry Flash, generating the work now gathered in this issue.  Our
        delay in getting the issue out, while unintentional, does allow us to
        renew attention to Larry's achievement now that a year of mourning
        has passed.  Thanks to all who responded to our invitations.

                                                                                       — The Editors

 
P A S S A G E S  5
 
 
 
            Biog. hard to repeat many times. Inconclusive and gusty
            as it's likely to be. The Descent Beckons. The sky is
            still right and left. Born palsied, I have prosthetic
            hands when it comes to mail etc. wired through rube-
            goldberg brains. Dont know wht prt of biog shd be booked.

                —Larry Eigner, Contributor's Note in Poems Now,
                         ed. Hettie Jones (Kulchur Press, 1966)
