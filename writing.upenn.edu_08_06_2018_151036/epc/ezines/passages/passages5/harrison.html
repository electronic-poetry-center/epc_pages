Homage to Larry Eigner
         

          cantilever bridge.  brought in

          windows often, for you shall

          remember flocks on grasses, breezes

          curled pencil lines your page

          fern.  glass through leaves in

          clouds Wide leaning over

          frames cast in.  legions eyed

          body of the sky, birdWing

          motions onward through my cell -

          sticks uneven, your trees tall

          with heavy rain.  a circle twice before

          your calm eyed face once clear

          in swimming leaves, still

          unraveling, sprawled out

          with ocean Cranes, under

          mirrors on your waves, under

          your ears.  you see with

          eyes in every sun and water ripples

          evening on my willow strings for you
         

                                                                    —Bob Harrison
